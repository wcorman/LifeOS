# Jiu Jitsu

## Why This Matters
Jiu Jitsu is something I care about deeply. I started training in 2020 and have been on and off for about 6 years. Even though I am still a white belt, that reflects inconsistency more than lack of interest or potential.

## Current Context
- Started training in 2020
- Training has been inconsistent over the years
- Still a white belt
- Competed in one tournament and learned a lot from it

## Tournament Reflection
I am proud that I competed.

### Match 1
- Spent the first minute standing and grip-fighting
- Felt reasonably good there
- Attempted an Uchi Mata
- The attempt got reversed somehow
- Ended up on bottom and struggled to escape
- Nearly got armbarred before time ran out

### Match 2
- Forgot to put in my mouth guard
- Went for a takedown
- Got caught in a headlock
- Panicked and tapped quickly because of the jaw / mouth pressure

## What Stands Out
What I need more of is not just class attendance, but more training that matches real resistance and match conditions.

### Skills to Improve
- Actual sparring
- Escapes
- Takedowns
- Calmness under pressure
- Competition readiness and routines

## Offensive Strengths / Opportunities
I often feel able to find a path to the back.

That suggests a strong offensive development path:
- improve back control
- improve submissions from the back
- build a reliable finishing system once back exposure is achieved

## Specific Technical Interests
- Bow and arrow choke as a high-percentage back submission
- Better use of the legs to create pressure rather than overusing the arms
- More efficient control and finishing mechanics

## Important Principles
- Use the legs to create pressure and control, not just the arms
- Train for efficiency, not panic
- Build skill through consistency
- Develop sustainable training habits instead of stop-start cycles

## Injury Context
I had a lower back injury from training a few years ago that was hard to recover from.

That experience matters. I want to train in a way that is sustainable for the long term, and I think Functional Patterns can help support that by improving movement quality, structure, and resilience.

## Current Focus
- Train more consistently
- Get more live rounds
- Improve escapes and takedowns
- Build a clearer back-attack system
- Protect the lower back and train sustainably
