# Jiu Jitsu - Tournament Lessons

## Why This Note Exists
Capture what the tournament revealed so the experience becomes useful, not just memorable.

## What I’m Proud Of
- I actually signed up and competed
- I tested myself under real pressure
- I learned where my current game holds up and where it breaks down

## Match 1 Lessons
### What happened
- Started standing and grip-fighting
- Felt reasonably solid there
- Attempted an Uchi Mata
- The attempt got reversed
- Ended up on bottom and stayed there for much of the match
- Nearly got armbarred before time expired

### What this suggests
- I need better follow-through and understanding on takedown entries
- I need stronger responses when a takedown attempt fails
- I need better bottom escapes under pressure

## Match 2 Lessons
### What happened
- Forgot to put in my mouth guard
- Went for a takedown
- Got caught in a headlock
- Panicked and tapped quickly because of the jaw / mouth pressure

### What this suggests
- Competition routines matter
- Small preparation mistakes can affect confidence fast
- I need more calm exposure to pressure-heavy positions
- I need better composure when things go wrong suddenly

## Main Training Gaps Revealed
- More actual sparring
- More escapes
- More takedown practice
- More comfort under live pressure
- Better competition preparation and routine

## Important Reminders
- Losing does not make the tournament a failure
- The real value is in turning exposure into direction
- Competing once already puts me ahead of where I would be if I stayed comfortable

## Next Improvements
- Build a pre-training / pre-competition checklist
- Practice more live rounds
- Focus on escaping bad positions calmly
- Sharpen takedown choices and follow-ups
- Keep building toward a back-attack game that fits me
