# Jiu Jitsu - Game Plan

## Current Priorities
- Train more consistently
- Get more actual sparring
- Improve escapes
- Improve takedowns
- Build confidence under pressure
- Improve submissions from the back

## Offensive Direction
A natural offensive path seems to be:
- find the back
- stabilize back control
- develop a reliable finishing system

## Back Attack Focus
- Seatbelt control
- Maintaining back position
- Controlling with the legs
- Finishing mechanics
- Staying patient rather than rushing the submission

## Submissions to Develop
- Bow and arrow choke
- Rear naked choke
- Collar-based finishes from the back

## Defensive / Survival Focus
- Escapes from bottom
- Escapes under pressure
- Staying calmer in bad positions
- Preventing panic reactions

## Standing / Takedown Focus
- Grip fighting
- Entries with commitment and balance
- Sharpening takedown attempts without overexposing position
- Understanding what went wrong in failed entries

## Principles
- Use the legs to create pressure and control
- Conserve the arms where possible
- Seek efficiency over force
- Train in a way that supports long-term durability

## Questions to Explore
- What is my highest-percentage route to the back?
- What are the most reliable finishes once I get there?
- Which escapes should become automatic first?
- What takedowns best fit my body and instincts?
