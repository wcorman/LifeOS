# Eagle Heights Estates - Client Onboarding Package

## Purpose
Create a simple, low-friction onboarding flow for Chris and the Eagle Heights team so they can easily provide assets, feedback, and input without getting overwhelmed.

The goal is to make it easy for them to contribute what is needed while keeping momentum high on the 3-week timeline.

## 1. Client Onboarding Checklist
Use this as the internal checklist for getting the client set up.

### Checklist
- [ ] Create shared Google Drive folder
- [ ] Create the main subfolders
- [ ] Add a feedback / copy guidance doc
- [ ] Add any current proposal / reference materials
- [ ] Send the onboarding email to Chris and team
- [ ] Ask for priority assets first
- [ ] Confirm who will provide final approval
- [ ] Confirm who will gather copy / facts / imagery internally
- [ ] Follow up quickly if uploads stall

## 2. Shared Folder Structure Plan
Suggested Google Drive structure:

- `01 - Logos & Branding`
- `02 - Property / Lot Images`
- `03 - Community / Area Images`
- `04 - Documents / Maps / Site Plans`
- `05 - Copy / Notes / Feedback`

### Notes on each folder
**01 - Logos & Branding**
- logo files
- brand colors
- any font references
- existing marketing materials

**02 - Property / Lot Images**
- lot photos
- development photos
- drone imagery
- progress photos

**03 - Community / Area Images**
- nearby area photos
- surrounding lifestyle imagery
- any visuals that help tell the location story

**04 - Documents / Maps / Site Plans**
- site plans
- lot maps
- pricing sheets
- development documents
- reference PDFs

**05 - Copy / Notes / Feedback**
- shared notes
- client messaging ideas
- questions and answers
- feedback on drafts

## 3. Client Copy / Feedback Questionnaire
Instead of asking them to write polished copy, use prompts that make it easy to react and contribute.

### Core Questions
- What should people understand about Eagle Heights within the first 10 seconds?
- What makes Eagle Heights different from other options?
- Why would someone choose to live here?
- What kind of buyer is the best fit?
- What do buyers seem to care about most?
- What questions do buyers ask most often?
- What language feels true to the project?
- What language feels wrong or too salesy?

### More Specific Questions
- What should we say about the location?
- What should we avoid saying about the location?
- What are the most important features of the lots?
- What level of detail should we share about pricing / availability?
- Are there any phrases or positioning ideas you especially like?
- Are there any words / claims you do not want used?

### Easy Feedback Prompt For Draft Reviews
When reviewing a page draft, ask them to comment on:
- what feels accurate
- what feels unclear
- what feels missing
- what feels off-brand
- what they would most want changed first

## 4. Draft Email To Chris And Team
Subject: Eagle Heights website onboarding and shared folder

Hi Chris,

I’m excited to get moving on the Eagle Heights Estates website.

To make the process easy and keep momentum strong, I’d like to use a shared Google Drive folder where your team can upload any assets, images, documents, and notes that will help support the build.

Inside the folder, I’ll organize things into a few simple sections for branding, lot/property imagery, community/area imagery, maps/documents, and copy/feedback.

I’d also love your team’s help with a few lightweight content prompts, not polished website copy, just your thoughts on things like:
- what people should understand about Eagle Heights right away
- what makes it different
- what buyers care about most
- what language feels right or wrong for the project

That will help me shape the first drafts in a way that feels true to the project without putting too much writing pressure on your side.

The main things that would help first are:
- any logos / brand assets
- lot or development imagery
- maps / site plans / pricing sheets
- any notes on key selling points or common buyer questions

Once I have those, I can move quickly into strong first drafts for the site.

Thanks, and I’m looking forward to building this with you.

Wes

## Notes
- Keep the ask simple and friendly.
- Do not ask them for too much at once.
- The goal is momentum, not perfection.
- This onboarding package should reduce back-and-forth and make the next steps obvious.
