# Keyword Research

## Research Summary
The strongest search intent appears to center on one core problem: people want to access their Obsidian vault from a browser, from another computer, or without installing the app.

The most promising keyword families are:
- browser access
- web version / web client
- remote access
- online vault access
- self-hosted or private access

There is also strong community-language overlap around phrases like:
- “Can I use Obsidian through a browser?”
- “How do I access my Obsidian notes online?”
- “I can’t install Obsidian on my work computer”
- “How do I access my synced vault through the browser?”

## Priority Keyword Clusters

### 1. Browser Access Keywords
These look like the highest-value targets because they map directly to LavaLink’s core value proposition.

Primary targets:
- obsidian in browser
- access obsidian vault from browser
- obsidian vault in browser
- open obsidian notes in browser
- browser access for obsidian
- access obsidian online

Why these matter:
- Strong product-intent
- Clear problem/solution match
- Likely to convert better than vague PKM traffic

### 2. Web Version / Web Client Keywords
A lot of users do not know the “right” term, so they search in plain language.

Primary targets:
- obsidian web version
- obsidian web app
- obsidian web client
- can i use obsidian through a browser
- obsidian browser version

Why these matter:
- High alignment with how users phrase the problem in Reddit/forums
- Good opportunity for landing pages, FAQ pages, and comparison content

### 3. Remote Access Keywords
These are strong because they capture users who are trying to solve access across devices or locations.

Primary targets:
- access obsidian remotely
- remote access obsidian vault
- access obsidian on another computer
- browse obsidian vault remotely
- how to access obsidian from another device

Why these matter:
- Good educational search traffic
- Good fit for blog posts and problem-solving content

### 4. Online Vault Access Keywords
These are useful because some searchers think in terms of “online notes” rather than browser-based access.

Primary targets:
- access obsidian vault online
- view obsidian notes online
- obsidian online access
- obsidian knowledge base online
- access markdown notes online

Why these matter:
- Broader top-of-funnel traffic
- Strong bridge from educational content into product pages

### 5. Self-Hosted / Private Access Keywords
These may be lower volume, but they are likely highly relevant for the right audience.

Primary targets:
- self-hosted obsidian web access
- private obsidian web access
- self-hosted obsidian browser access
- access obsidian privately online
- self-hosted markdown knowledge base browser

Why these matter:
- Higher fit for technical, ownership-minded users
- Good angle for trust, privacy, and control messaging

## High-Intent Phrases to Work Into Copy
These are likely worth using directly in titles, H1s, subheads, FAQs, and Reddit replies:
- Access your Obsidian vault from any browser
- Open your Obsidian notes online
- Use Obsidian from a browser without installing the app
- Access your Obsidian vault on your work computer
- Remotely access your Obsidian vault
- Browser-based access for your Obsidian vault
- A web client for your Obsidian vault

## Community-Language Findings
The most useful real-world phrasing seems to come from questions like:
- “Can I use Obsidian through a browser?”
- “Is there a web version of Obsidian?”
- “How do I access my Obsidian notes at work?”
- “How do I access my vault remotely?”
- “How do I access a synced vault through the browser?”

These are strong because they reveal both the keywords and the emotional context:
- need access from work
- cannot install local software
- want continuity across devices
- do not want to abandon Obsidian

## Content Opportunities
- Create one page per intent cluster instead of forcing everything into one page
- Match landing page copy to the exact language users use in Reddit and search
- Turn recurring objections into FAQ sections
- Use community wording in subheads, not just SEO metadata
- Build pages around the user’s situation, not just product features

## Next Actions
- Prioritize one landing page around “Obsidian in browser”
- Write one educational article around “How to access your Obsidian vault online”
- Create one FAQ around work computer / no install / remote access scenarios
- Review Reddit threads and log the exact wording users repeat most often

## Notes
This keyword space appears to be more intent-rich than volume-rich. That is probably fine. The goal is not to chase generic PKM traffic — it is to capture users with a very specific problem that LavaLink actually solves.
